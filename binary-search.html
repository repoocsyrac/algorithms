Binary Search

Key facts

  • Goal: Finds specific element in a sorted list.
  • Best-case time complexity: O(1)
  • Worst-case time complexity: O(log N)
  • Average time complexity: O(log N)
  • Space complexity: O(1)
  • Pros: Faster than linear search.
  • Cons: List must be sorted.
  • When to use: When you are searching a very large list.

How it works

We start at the midpoint of the list, and check whether the item at this index is the item we are looking for.

If so, we are done.

If the required item is larger than the element at the midpoint, we discard all the elements up to and including the midpoint, and repeat.

If the required item is smaller than the element at the midpoint, we discard the midpoint and all elements after it, and repeat.
